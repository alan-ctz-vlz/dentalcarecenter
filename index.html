Non-surgical treatments

Scaling and root planing

Also known as conventional periodontal therapy, is a deep cleaning below the gumline used to treat gum disease. Scaling is when your dentist removes all the plaque and tartar. Then, root planing, helps your gums reattach to your teeth.

Surgical treatments

Pocket Depth Reduction

The periodontist folds back the gum tissue and removes the disease-causing bacteria. It’s Important to prevent damaged caused by periodontal disease.

Regeneration

These procedures can reverse some of the damage by regenerating lost bone and tissue. The bone supporting your teeth has been destroyed due to periodontal disease.

Soft-Tissue Graft

This procedure helps when tooth roots are exposed as a result of gum recession. Gum graft will repair the defect and help to prevent additional recession and bone loss.


Crown Lengthening

The periodontist could perform this procedure to correct the length of your gum tissue improving your smile. Could be a restorative or cosmetic.
